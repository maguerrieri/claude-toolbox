# Ironsworn Core Moves

These are the curated core moves for Ironsworn. Each move triggers on a specific fictional action. Rolls use the base engine defined in `ironsworn-core/adapter.md`: `roll ironsworn-action --stat <stat> --adds <N>`.

---

## Adventure Moves

### Face Danger
**Trigger:** When you attempt something risky or react to an imminent threat.
**Roll:** +edge (to act quickly or avoid), +heart (to resist fear or stay resolute), +iron (to endure or power through), +shadow (to act with stealth or deception), or +wits (to find a way out or exercise caution) — whichever best fits the action.

- **Strong hit:** You handle it. Proceed.
- **Weak hit:** You succeed, but face a troublesome cost — a lesser harm, complication, or difficult choice.
- **Miss:** You fail and things get worse. Pay the Price.

---

### Secure an Advantage
**Trigger:** When you assess a situation, make preparations, or bolster your position before acting.
**Roll:** +edge (survey quickly), +heart (give heart to allies), +iron (brace for effort), +shadow (read the angles), or +wits (study the situation).

- **Strong hit:** Gain +2 momentum. Take +1 add on your next roll if the advantage still applies.
- **Weak hit:** Gain +1 momentum.
- **Miss:** Your advantage is lost or turns against you. Pay the Price.

---

### Gather Information
**Trigger:** When you search an area, delve into lore, or examine clues.
**Roll:** +wits.

- **Strong hit:** You learn something valuable. Take +2 momentum.
- **Weak hit:** You find something, but it's incomplete or raises new questions. Take +1 momentum.
- **Miss:** You find little useful, and the search draws danger. Pay the Price.

---

### Compel
**Trigger:** When you try to persuade, bargain with, or coerce someone.
**Roll:** +heart (appeal through trust or emotion), +iron (threaten or intimidate), or +shadow (manipulate or deceive).

- **Strong hit:** They do what you ask. Take +1 momentum.
- **Weak hit:** They comply, but with conditions, reservations, or a cost.
- **Miss:** They refuse, and the situation worsens. Pay the Price.

## Combat Moves

### Strike
**Trigger:** When you attack an enemy at close quarters.
**Roll:** +iron (force and might) or +edge (speed and precision).

- **Strong hit:** Inflict harm as described for your weapon (+2), and take +1 momentum.
- **Weak hit:** Inflict harm as described, but the enemy retaliates or you find yourself in a worse position.
- **Miss:** Your attack fails and you are in danger. Pay the Price.

---

### Clash
**Trigger:** When you fight back against an attacking enemy.
**Roll:** +iron or +edge.

- **Strong hit:** Inflict harm (+2) and take +1 momentum. You gain the upper hand.
- **Weak hit:** Inflict harm, but you must Endure Harm as well — the exchange costs both sides.
- **Miss:** The exchange goes against you. Pay the Price.

---

### Turn the Tide
**Trigger:** When you seize a dramatic reversal against a foe who has the upper hand.
*(This move does not require a roll.)*

- You may act before the enemy and make a move — add +1. On a hit, inflict +1 harm or take +1 momentum, and take +1 momentum overall.

---

### Endure Harm
**Trigger:** When you face physical injury or hardship.
**Roll:** +iron or +health (whichever is lower).

- **Strong hit:** Choose: take -1 health but press on, or take -2 health but gain +1 momentum (you draw strength from pain).
- **Weak hit:** Take -1 health. You can continue.
- **Miss:** Take -2 health. It's worse than expected — if health is now 0, you must Face Death or Face Desolation.

---

### Face Death
**Trigger:** When you are brought to the brink of death (health reaches 0 and you suffer additional harm).
**Roll:** +heart.

- **Strong hit:** You survive by a hair. Take 1 health and continue, deeply shaken.
- **Weak hit:** Somehow you endure, but something is lost or changed forever. Work with the GM.
- **Miss:** You die, or you must make a desperate bargain to avoid it. The cost is enormous.

---

## Vow Moves

### Swear an Iron Vow
**Trigger:** When you swear a vow to a person or community, or commit to a personal quest.
**Roll:** +heart.
Set a rank: troublesome (2 progress/advance), dangerous (1.5), formidable (1), extreme (0.5), or epic (0.25).

- **Strong hit:** You are resolved. Take +2 momentum.
- **Weak hit:** You are determined, but the path is uncertain. Take +1 momentum.
- **Miss:** Something threatens your commitment from the start. Pay the Price, and still set the vow.

---

### Reach a Milestone
**Trigger:** When you accomplish a significant step toward a vow's fulfilment.
*(No roll.)*

- Mark progress on the vow's track according to its rank. Describe what was achieved.

---

### Fulfil Your Vow
**Trigger:** When you believe you have achieved what your vow demands (progress move).
**Roll:** Roll the challenge dice only against your progress track's filled boxes.

- **Strong hit:** Your vow is fulfilled. Take +2 experience.
- **Weak hit:** Your vow is fulfilled, but there is a complication, cost, or lingering consequence. Take +1 experience.
- **Miss:** Your vow is not yet fulfilled — or the cost of completing it is greater than expected. Pay the Price.

---

## Recovery Moves

### Sojourn
**Trigger:** When you spend time at a settlement recovering, seeking aid, or resupplying.
**Roll:** +heart.

- **Strong hit:** Choose two: **Recuperate** (take +2 health for you and any companions), **Provision** (+2 supply), **Plan** (+2 momentum), **Relate** (make a Forge a Bond move without the roll).
- **Weak hit:** Choose one of the above.
- **Miss:** You find little comfort here, and something complicates the stay. Pay the Price.

---

## Journey Moves

### Undertake a Journey
**Trigger:** When you travel by land, sea, or through the wilds to a distant destination.
**Roll:** +wits. Set a rank for the journey; each strong or weak hit marks progress.

- **Strong hit:** Mark progress and take +1 momentum. You reach a waypoint — describe it.
- **Weak hit:** Mark progress, but you face a peril, delay, or cost (lose -1 supply, endure harm, or face danger).
- **Miss:** Something goes seriously wrong. Do not mark progress. Pay the Price.

When progress is complete, make a progress roll (challenge dice vs. filled boxes) to resolve the journey's end.
